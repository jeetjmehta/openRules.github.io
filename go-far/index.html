Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation facts What is Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation for? Find prostate cancer at early stage and before it spreads beyond the prostate Who should consider Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation? Men aged 50 to 69 years Earlier (45 years) in men who are at increased risk for prostate cancer (African American race and a first-degree relative diagnosed with prostate cancer) Who should NOT undergo Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation? Men older than 70 years or with life expectancy of less than 10 years. Also, if you are concerned that you would be uncomfortable knowing that you have cancer and not treating it, screening may not be for you Who recommends Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation? The American College of Physicians recommends screening (see reference 1). The USPSTF does not recommend screening (see reference 2) Other things to consider doing Do not treat a cancer that is found but does not have signs of aggression

Did the test help?
Less men died of prostate cancer; however studies disagree on how many less men.
• According to the ERSPC trial, 1 death was prevented among 1000 men screened for 9 years (reference 3)
• According to the Göteborg trial, 1 death was prevented among 300 men screened for 14 years (reference 4)
